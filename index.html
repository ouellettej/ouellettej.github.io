HACTC Curriclulm Design

I joined the HACTC as an instructor in February of 2022 and was tasked with restarting their Information Technology program, which had been dormant for several years. I undertook a complete redesign of the curriculum informed by meetings with local employers and postsecondary education providers. The resulting curriculum thoroughly prepares students for immediate career success or further education and training in the IT industry.

For more details see the program pages for my Information Technology and Software Engineering classes on the HACTC website. The IT program was also featured in the Fall/Winter issue of Valley Parents.

MNIST GUI

I wrote this tool as a framework for students to learn the basics of neural networks in an artificial intelligence course I taught at Vermont Technical College. The tool allows users to view how parameters such as batch sizes and learning rate affect how quickly the network learns to recognize digits.

The source code is published in the mnist-gui project on my GitHub page.

Research

Before my teaching career, I studied space plasma physics using global-scale computer simulations. My work focused on the phenomenon of magnetic reconnection, where opposing magnetic fields embedded in a plasma merge and convert their stored magnetic energy into kinetic energy. The ultimate goal of this work was to better understand how reconnection shapes mangetospheric dynamics and to improve space weather prediction models.
